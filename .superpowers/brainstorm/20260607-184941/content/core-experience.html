Modeling and Predicting Smartphone Battery Life Under Realistic Usage Patterns

Smartphone battery life is strongly affected by user behavior, network conditions and workload characteristics. This paper presents a practical modeling approach based on observed energy use across common scenarios.

The proposed method combines device telemetry with workload-aware prediction. Results show that contextual signals improve estimation accuracy and provide a useful basis for adaptive power management.

Experiments were performed under controlled and real-world conditions. The resulting model remains lightweight enough for local inference while preserving interpretable factors.
